# Background: Artificial Wisdom (AW)

Artificial Wisdom (AW) is proposed as a higher-order framework for aligning AI capability with wisdom, natural law, long-term stability, reversibility, accountability, ecological consistency, and regenerative capacity.

## Why This Framework Exists

Artificial Intelligence can increase speed, scale, automation, and capability.

However, capability alone does not guarantee wisdom. If an intelligent system optimizes only for short-term success, profit, efficiency, or automation, it can amplify systemic risk.

AW asks intelligent systems to evaluate not only whether something can be done, but whether it should be done, under what limits, and with what long-term consequences.

## Relationship to Prior Uses of “Artificial Wisdom”

The phrase “Artificial Wisdom” has appeared in broader philosophical and AI discussions.

This repository does not claim first use of the phrase itself.

Instead, it defines and publishes a specific Natural-Law-Based Artificial Wisdom Framework built on:

- the Six Principles,
- Natural Law,
- Wa-Node thinking,
- civilization OS concepts,
- AI tuning,
- and the Artificial Wisdom Guardrail Protocol.

## Core Difference Between AI and AW

```text
Artificial Intelligence asks: Can this be done?
Artificial Wisdom asks: Should this be done, under what limits, and with what long-term consequences?
```
